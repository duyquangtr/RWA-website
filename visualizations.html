Visualizing RWA Liquidity, Capital Allocation, and Market Structure

The following dashboard walks through our four-part analytical framework for understanding how macroeconomic liquidity, yield environments, and multichain deployment shape the evolution of the Real-World Asset (RWA) sector. Each section pairs narrative explanation with visual evidence to show how capital enters the system, rotates between asset types, spreads across blockchains, and ultimately accumulates across RWA protocols.

1. Macro Liquidity & RWA Sector Expansion

The Macro Liquidity vs RWA TVL chart shows that as stablecoin supply and on-chain liquidity expanded, the entire RWA sector grew rapidly rather than concentrating in one or two dominant protocols. Early in the timeline, RWA adoption was small, so TVL was heavily concentrated in pioneers like Centrifuge and Maple. But once stablecoin liquidity surged in 2023–2025, significantly more capital flowed into the ecosystem, enabling a wider range of protocols to attract deposits. This broad-based liquidity expansion reduced overall concentration, which is reflected in the declining HHI values over time.

2. Yield Regimes & Asset Allocation Dynamics

The Capital Allocation by Asset Type chart reveals major shifts in investor preference across Diversified, Private Credit, and Treasury-backed RWAs. When the U.S. 10-Year Treasury yield was low, private credit dominated, concentrating TVL in credit-focused protocols like Maple and Goldfinch. However, as the 10-Year yield climbed in 2023–2025, capital rotated sharply into tokenized Treasuries, allowing newly launched Treasury-focused platforms (e.g., Ondo Finance, Superstate) to capture substantial market share. This yield-driven reallocation diversified the RWA landscape and directly reduced protocol concentration, contributing to the downward trend observed in the HHI index.

Yield Regimes vs RWA Asset Mix

Shows how the composition of RWA TVL across Private Credit, Treasury, and Diversified assets shifts with changes in the U.S. 10-Year Treasury yield. This reveals how investors dynamically reallocate capital in response to shifting macro incentives.

Analysis

Shifts in the 10 Year Treasury yield correspond to rotations in investor allocation. Low yields push capital toward higher return private credit platforms, while rising yields increase demand for safer tokenized Treasury products. Changing macro incentives reshape the balance of RWA asset types and diversify the overall market.

3. Multichain Expansion & Capital Distribution

The Chain Allocation Over Time chart shows the RWA market expanding from primarily Ethereum-based protocols to multiple chains, including Solana, Avalanche, Mantle, Plume, and XRPL. As more chains became viable environments for tokenized assets, new RWA protocols emerged natively on these chains or migrated to them, breaking the early dominance of a few Ethereum-only platforms. This multi-chain diversification lowered barriers to entry and enabled more protocols to accumulate meaningful TVL. As a result, the protocol concentration decreased steadily, which is captured clearly in the Altair HHI line chart.

Chain Allocation Over Time

A stacked time series showing how RWA TVL is distributed across major blockchains. This helps illustrate the sector's transition from Ethereum dominance to a more decentralized, multi-chain footprint.

Analysis

Early RWA growth was concentrated on Ethereum, but TVL gradually expanded to Solana, Avalanche, Mantle, and other chains. The movement toward a multichain footprint reduces dependence on a single network, enables faster scaling, and distributes settlement risk across different ecosystems.

4. Protocol-Level Market Structure & Concentration

The Yield Regimes vs Asset Composition visualization shows that RWA investors responded strongly to shifting Treasury yield environments. Rising yields created a boom in tokenized Treasury products, which directly enabled new specialized protocols to grow. While early RWA TVL was concentrated in private-credit-focused platforms, the yield-driven surge in Treasury demand redistributed TVL across a broader set of competitors. This yield-induced rotation reduced market concentration and produced the gradual HHI decline (section 4) seen in the Altair chart, especially during 2023-2025.

Given the steady decline in concentration shown in the HHI chart and the continued diversification highlighted across all four D3 visualizations, the near-term trend points toward an increasingly competitive and multi-protocol RWA ecosystem. With Treasury yields stabilizing and more institutions entering the tokenization space, new specialized platforms are likely to emerge, especially those focused on institutional-grade issuance, real-time settlement, and multi-chain interoperability. Simultaneously, established players will continue expanding to new chains and asset classes. As a result, protocol concentration is expected to remain relatively low, reflecting a maturing market where liquidity is distributed across a broader set of issuers, chains, and RWA product types.
