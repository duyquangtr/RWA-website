Visualizing RWA Liquidity, Capital Allocation, and Market Structure

The following dashboard walks through our four-part analytical framework for understanding how macroeconomic liquidity, yield environments, and multichain deployment shape the evolution of the Real-World Asset (RWA) sector. Each section pairs narrative explanation with visual evidence to show how capital enters the system, rotates between asset types, spreads across blockchains, and ultimately accumulates across RWA protocols.

1. Macro Liquidity & RWA Sector Expansion

RWA adoption begins with liquidity. Stablecoins serve as the primary settlement asset across DeFi, and their total supply closely tracks the amount of “deployable capital” available for RWAs. When M2 money supply and stablecoin liquidity expand, investors have more on-chain dollars to allocate—fueling broad-based increases in RWA TVL. Early in the sector, TVL was small and concentrated in a few pioneer protocols. As liquidity surged in 2023–2025, growth accelerated across multiple platforms, laying the foundation for a more competitive ecosystem.

RWA sector growth is strongly linked to stablecoin liquidity. As total stablecoin market capitalization rises, RWA TVL scales alongside it—indicating that stablecoins function as a leading indicator for RWA expansion.

Quantifies the relationships between RWA TVL and macro factors such as stablecoin supply, M2, and Treasury yields. Strong correlations reinforce the central role of liquidity in driving RWA adoption.

2. Yield Regimes & Asset Allocation Dynamics

Interest rates determine the relative attractiveness of different RWA categories. When Treasury yields are low, investors favor private credit protocols that offer higher returns, leading to TVL concentration in credit-focused platforms. As Treasury yields rise, capital rotates into safer, tokenized Treasury products— enabling new entrants like Ondo Finance and Superstate to capture significant market share. This rotation diversifies the RWA landscape and reduces reliance on a small number of private-credit protocols.

Yield Regimes vs RWA Asset Mix

Shows how the composition of RWA TVL across Private Credit, Treasury, and Diversified assets shifts with changes in the U.S. 10-Year Treasury yield. This reveals how investors dynamically reallocate capital in response to shifting macro incentives.

3. Multichain Expansion & Capital Distribution

The RWA ecosystem originally launched on Ethereum, but as the sector matured, protocols expanded across multiple chains—from Solana and Avalanche to Mantle and newer RWA-focused networks. Multichain deployment lowers barriers to entry for new protocols, enables faster scaling, and reduces concentration risk by distributing TVL across different settlement environments.

Chain Allocation Over Time

A stacked time series showing how RWA TVL is distributed across major blockchains. This helps illustrate the sector's transition from Ethereum dominance to a more decentralized, multi-chain footprint.

4. Protocol-Level Market Structure & Concentration

Combining the effects of liquidity expansion, asset-type rotation, and multichain deployment helps explain how the protocol landscape evolved. Early RWA TVL was concentrated in a handful of private-credit protocols. As new liquidity entered the system and more chains became viable deployment targets, protocol-level competition increased and market share spread more evenly across platforms.

While the top three protocols still hold a majority of TVL, the Herfindahl–Hirschman Index (HHI) shows a clear downward trend, indicating decreasing concentration and a healthier, more competitive ecosystem.
